DANGEROUS OCCURRENCE & INJURY REPORT
Location: Bhilai Steel Plant (SAIL), Chhattisgarh
Date of Incident: October 8, 2023
Affected Unit: Blast Furnace Gas Cleaning Plant (GCP)
Injuries: 3 Workers Hospitalized (Acute CO Toxicity)

INCIDENT DESCRIPTION:
During flange tightening maintenance near Blast Furnace #8 GCP, a sudden surge in Blast Furnace gas (rich in Carbon Monoxide and Hydrocarbons) escaped from a corroded gasket. Simultaneously, a contractor crew was performing welding (Hot Work PTW #HW-881) only 12 meters away on an elevated pipeline structure.

GAS READINGS AT INCIDENT TIME:
- CO Concentration: Spiked to 320 ppm within 90 seconds.
- Methane/Hydrocarbon (% LEL): Registered 35% LEL.
- Distance to Ignition Source: 12 meters (Violation of mandatory 15-meter buffer).

CONTRIBUTING FACTORS:
1. Simultaneous Adjacent Operations (SIMOPS): Hot work permit was approved without verifying downstream flange integrity in the adjacent zone.
2. Lack of Spatial Risk Mapping: Safety officers evaluated work requests on a zone-by-zone isolation basis without calculating radial proximity hazards across zone borders.

REGULATORY BREACH:
OISD-STD-018 Clause 8.1 strictly prohibits hot work within 15 meters of any potential hydrocarbon or flammable gas release source without positive mechanical blanking.
